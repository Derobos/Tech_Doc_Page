Linux installation guide

Choosing a distribution
Some tips to help you choose the right distribution of Linux.
  1. Determine the specifications for the computer you want to use.
  2. Make note of the CPU, amount of RAM (memory), and storage (HDD or SSD) available.
  3. Research which distributions will work best for your computer
  4. You may also be able to choose from a selection of desktop styles.

Linux is an open source operating system. This means that it is free to use, modify, or distribute.

That offers a lot of freedom for users, programmers, and developers to customize their operating system.

There are many additional benefits such as less preloaded "bloatware" and more privacy.

This can all be confusing for new comers. Guides and other help can be found online within the Linux community.
